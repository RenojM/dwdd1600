Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence (1776) include

John Hancock
President of the Continental Congress and best known for his large, bold signature on the Declaration of Independence, which became synonymous with the word “signature.”
Thomas Jefferson
The primary author of the Declaration of Independence and later served as the third President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin
A renowned inventor, writer, and diplomat who played a crucial role in gaining French support for the American Revolution.
John Adams
A strong advocate for independence in the Continental Congress and later became the second President of the United States.
Samuel Adams
A key organizer of revolutionary resistance and a leader of the Sons of Liberty who helped mobilize colonial opposition to British rule.
Roger Sherman
The only individual to sign the Continental Association, the Declaration of Independence, the Articles of Confederation, and the U.S. Constitution.
Charles Carroll of Carrollton
The wealthiest signer of the Declaration of Independence who supported independence despite the significant personal risk involved.
